Common Questions About Therapy

Find answers to common questions about therapy, my services, and what to expect. If your question isn't addressed here, please don't hesitate to contact me directly.

Getting Started

What can I expect during my first session?

Your first session is primarily about getting to know each other and establishing a foundation for our work together. We'll discuss what brought you to therapy, explore your background and current concerns, and identify your goals for treatment.

This initial meeting also provides an opportunity for you to ask questions about my approach and determine if we're a good fit for working together. I'll explain confidentiality, my policies, and what you can expect from the therapeutic process.

While we'll begin to address your concerns in the first session, the primary focus is on building rapport and gathering information to develop an effective treatment plan. Many people find that simply talking about their concerns in a supportive environment provides some immediate relief.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Therapy can be beneficial for anyone seeking personal growth, improved well-being, or help with specific mental health concerns. You might consider therapy if:

  • You're experiencing persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or emotional distress
  • You're struggling to cope with major life changes or transitions
  • Your relationships are causing significant stress or conflict
  • You're having difficulty managing stress or finding balance in your life
  • Past experiences or traumas are impacting your current functioning
  • You're seeking greater self-understanding or personal growth

The decision to begin therapy is personal, and many people find it helpful to schedule an initial consultation to explore whether therapy aligns with their needs and goals.

How do I schedule an appointment?

Scheduling an appointment is simple. You can:

  • Fill out the contact form on this website
  • Call my office directly at (555) 123-4567
  • Email me at catherine@catherinelambert.com

When you reach out, please include your name, contact information, and a brief note about what you're seeking help with. For new clients, I'll typically get back to you within 24-48 business hours to schedule an initial consultation.

The Therapy Process

How long does each therapy session last?

Standard therapy sessions are 50 minutes long, which is considered a therapeutic hour. Initial consultations may be scheduled for 60-90 minutes to allow for a more comprehensive assessment of your needs and concerns.

For certain therapeutic approaches or specific needs, longer sessions may occasionally be recommended and arranged in advance. I maintain strict time boundaries to ensure all clients receive their full scheduled time.

How often will we meet?

Most clients begin with weekly sessions, which provides consistent support and momentum for change. This frequency allows us to build rapport, maintain continuity, and make steady progress toward your goals.

As you progress in therapy, we may adjust the frequency to biweekly or monthly sessions for maintenance and continued growth. In some cases, particularly during periods of crisis or when working through intense issues, twice-weekly sessions might be recommended.

We'll collaboratively determine the schedule that best meets your needs, taking into account your goals, current life circumstances, and practical considerations.

How long will I need to be in therapy?

The duration of therapy varies significantly depending on several factors:

  • The nature and complexity of the issues you're addressing
  • Your specific goals for therapy
  • Your personal history and current life circumstances
  • The pace at which you're comfortable working
  • Your engagement with the therapeutic process

Some clients achieve their goals in 8-12 sessions of focused, solution-oriented work. Others benefit from longer-term therapy to address deeper patterns or complex issues, which might extend to several months or even a year or more.

We'll regularly review your progress and adjust our approach as needed. Therapy is not intended to create dependency, but rather to foster your independence and well-being, so we'll work toward completion when you've achieved your goals.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule a session?

I understand that life circumstances sometimes require schedule changes. My cancellation policy requires 24 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling to avoid being charged for the session. This policy allows me to potentially offer that time to other clients who may be waiting for an appointment.

To cancel or reschedule, please call my office at (555) 123-4567 or email me at catherine@catherinelambert.com as soon as you know you need to make a change. For after-hours notifications, please leave a voicemail.

Emergencies and sudden illnesses are evaluated on a case-by-case basis. I make every effort to reschedule within the same week when possible.

Practical Matters

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, I accept several major insurance plans, including:

  • Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Aetna
  • Cigna
  • United Healthcare

I also offer self-pay options and sliding scale fees for those without insurance coverage or who prefer not to use their insurance. Please contact me to verify your specific insurance benefits or discuss payment options.

If you plan to use insurance, it's helpful to contact your insurance provider before our first session to understand your mental health benefits, including copays, deductibles, and any session limits.

Is everything I share confidential?

Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy and essential for creating a safe environment. What you share remains private with few legal and ethical exceptions, which include:

  • Situations involving imminent risk of harm to yourself or others
  • Suspected abuse or neglect of children, elderly persons, or vulnerable adults
  • Court orders requiring disclosure of information
  • Insurance billing requirements (typically limited to diagnosis and dates of service)

If you provide written consent, I may also share information with other healthcare providers to coordinate your care. We'll discuss these limitations in detail during your first session, and I'm happy to address any concerns you have about confidentiality.

Do you offer telehealth/virtual sessions?

Yes, I offer secure video sessions through a HIPAA-compliant telehealth platform. Telehealth provides a convenient option for clients who:

  • Live far from my office or have transportation limitations
  • Have busy schedules that make in-person appointments challenging
  • Prefer the comfort and privacy of engaging in therapy from home
  • Have health concerns that make remote sessions preferable

Virtual sessions maintain the same level of confidentiality and therapeutic quality as in-person meetings. Most insurance plans now cover telehealth services, though coverage varies, so it's recommended to verify this with your provider.

For optimal telehealth experiences, you'll need a private space, a reliable internet connection, and a device with a camera and microphone. I'm happy to provide technical guidance before our first virtual session.

What if therapy isn't working for me?

If you feel therapy isn't meeting your needs, I encourage you to discuss your concerns with me directly. Often, addressing these concerns can lead to adjustments in our approach that better align with your goals and preferences.

Therapeutic change isn't always linear or immediately noticeable. Sometimes what feels like a lack of progress is actually an important part of the process. Regular check-ins about your therapy experience help ensure we're on the right track.

If after discussion we determine that a different therapist or approach might better serve you, I'm happy to provide appropriate referrals. The most important factor is finding the right fit for your needs, even if that means working with another professional.
