Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about therapy and my services

What can I expect during my first session?
Your first session is primarily about getting to know each other. We'll discuss what brought you to therapy, your background and current concerns, and your goals for treatment. It's also an opportunity for you to ask questions and determine if we're a good fit for working together.
How long does each therapy session last?
Standard therapy sessions are 50 minutes long. Initial consultations may be scheduled for 60-90 minutes to allow for a more comprehensive assessment of your needs.
How often will we meet?
Most clients begin with weekly sessions, which provides consistent support and momentum for change. As you progress, we may adjust the frequency to biweekly or monthly sessions. We'll collaboratively determine the schedule that best meets your needs.
How long will I need to be in therapy?
The duration of therapy varies depending on your goals, the complexity of the issues you're addressing, and your pace of change. Some clients achieve their goals in 8-12 sessions, while others benefit from longer-term support. We'll regularly review your progress and adjust our approach as needed.
Do you accept insurance?
Yes, I accept several major insurance plans. I also offer self-pay options and sliding scale fees for those without insurance coverage. Please contact me to verify your specific insurance benefits.
Is everything I share confidential?
Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. What you share remains private with few exceptions, which include situations involving risk of harm to yourself or others, suspected abuse of children or vulnerable adults, or if required by court order. We'll discuss these limitations in detail during your first session.
